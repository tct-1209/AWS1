# Event 1: Event Meet Up

### Event information

| Item | Information |
|---|---|
| **Event name** | Event Meet Up |
| **Time** | 09:00, 30/05/2026 |
| **Location** | 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City |
| **Role** | Attendee |

### Event purpose

The Meet Up created an opportunity for FCAJ members to connect, listen to speaker sharing sessions, and broaden their perspectives on AWS, learning skills, hackathons, DevOps, and AI-powered applications. It also helped me observe how speakers organize ideas, share practical experiences, and connect technology knowledge with real products.

### Main content

#### 1. Huynh Thai Linh - What Beginners Worry About in AWS

This session focused on common concerns for AWS beginners, such as cost management, billing checks, resource cleanup, and avoiding unexpected charges. It helped me build safer habits when practicing cloud services.

#### 2. The Ballers - Hackathon Experience

The Ballers shared their hackathon journey, from idea development and task division to bug fixing and product completion under time pressure. I learned more about teamwork, communication, and presentation skills in a technology competition.

#### 3. Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu - Why We Always Need Confidence

This presentation emphasized confidence in learning, communication, and personal development. It reminded me that confidence helps learners ask questions, try new things, and present their results more actively.

#### 4. Tran Minh Quan - The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

The speaker discussed hidden work behind a project, including environment configuration, deployment, logging, monitoring, and incident preparation. I gained a clearer understanding that DevOps is essential for stable system operation.

#### 5. Khac Uy - A Familiar Enemy

This session discussed procrastination in study and work. It helped me reflect on time management, task breakdown, and the importance of starting early instead of waiting until deadlines.

#### 6. Tran Huu Nghia - AI-powered website introduction

The final sharing introduced an AI-powered website that interprets numerology-style results for users. The idea showed how AI can be integrated into a website to create a personalized and interactive experience.

### Outcomes

After the event, I gained a better understanding of safe AWS learning habits, cost awareness, teamwork, confidence, and the role of DevOps in product deployment. The event also motivated me to learn more proactively and present my work more clearly.
