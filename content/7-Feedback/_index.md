# Sharing and Feedback

## 7.1. General Reflection

The internship at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** gave me a more practical view of cloud computing and how AWS services are used in a complete system. Instead of only studying theory, I practiced with many service groups such as IAM, VPC, EC2, RDS, S3, monitoring, CI/CD, and cost optimization.

What impressed me most was that the program encouraged interns to research independently, complete labs, record errors, and improve their own reports. Through this process, I understood that learning cloud requires initiative, carefulness, and persistence.

## 7.2. What I Was Most Satisfied With

I was most satisfied with the way the program combined knowledge learning, hands-on labs, and community events. The content was practical, helping me understand concepts and apply them directly in the AWS environment.

In addition, the support from mentors and the community made me more confident when facing technical issues. Instead of receiving only final answers, I was guided to check problems step by step and understand the root causes.

## 7.3. What I Learned

After the internship, I learned how to approach a cloud system layer by layer: account and permissions, networking, compute, database, storage, security, monitoring, and cost management. I also realized that making a system run is not enough; it should also be stable, secure, and cost-controlled.

Besides technical knowledge, I also improved my note-taking, documentation, reporting, presentation, and weekly progress management skills.

## 7.4. Suggestions for Improvement

In my opinion, the program could provide a short weekly checklist so interns can easily track what to learn, which labs to complete, and what outcomes are expected. This would make the self-learning process clearer and more structured.

In addition, a short review session after each major topic such as Networking, Compute, Database, Security, or DevOps would help interns systematize knowledge and better understand how services connect with one another.

## 7.5. Answers to Some Questions

**What were you most satisfied with during the internship?**  
I was most satisfied with learning AWS through hands-on practice, working with many services, and developing the habit of troubleshooting issues instead of only following fixed instructions.

**What should the program improve for future interns?**  
I think the program could provide weekly checklists, a collection of common errors, and resource cleanup guidance after each lab.

**Would you recommend this internship to your friends? Why?**  
Yes. I would recommend it because the program is suitable for students who want to learn cloud through practice, receive community support, and build a complete report or project.

**Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  
I suggest adding short periodic review sessions, a lab note template, and billing-check guidance to help interns avoid unnecessary costs.

**Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes. If I have the opportunity, I would like to continue learning more about DevOps, cloud security, monitoring, and AWS architecture design.

## 7.6. Appreciation

I sincerely thank FCAJ, the mentors, and the community members for supporting me throughout the internship. The knowledge and experience I gained helped me become more confident when working with AWS and gave me clearer direction for developing my Cloud/DevOps skills.
